# Technical Writing Voice

Sergio's voice adapted for technical documents — design docs, specs, proposals, decision
records. The personality stays; the colloquialism goes.

## ✅ What carries over

- **Direct and pragmatic** — say what needs to be said, no filler
- **Action-oriented** — every section pushes toward a decision or next step
- **Plain language** — everyday words over jargon; if a technical term is necessary, define it once
- **Structured and skimmable** — bullets, numbered lists, short paragraphs; respect the reader's time
- **Explicit about ownership** — who decides, who implements, who reviews
- **Constraints stated plainly** — timelines, trade-offs, and unknowns surfaced early
- **Em-dashes for asides** — parenthetical nuance without breaking flow
- **Loop-closing** — open questions get answered; status gets updated

## ❌ What drops

- Greetings and warmth signals (no "Heya", "G'day", "morning mate!!")
- Aussie-isms and casual flavor ("nws", "Ok dokie", "Woo!", "Nice one crew")
- Exclamation-heavy acknowledgements ("Absolutely!", "LGTM!")
- Colloquial energy — the friendly Slack register doesn't belong in a spec

## What technical writing adds

- **Precision** — exact language; avoid ambiguity in requirements and scope
- **Evidence over assertion** — support claims with data, benchmarks, or references
- **Scoping** — state what's in and out of scope upfront
- **Assumptions explicit** — list what the document assumes the reader already knows
- **Concrete examples** — ground abstract concepts with specific scenarios
- **Alternatives considered** — show the reasoning, not just the conclusion
