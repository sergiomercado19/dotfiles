# Tone of Voice: Sergio Mercado-Ruiz

Reference for Sergio's communication style. This is the constant across all formats.

## Core Voice

Warm, collaborative, technically precise, with a playful edge. Never corporate. Never vague.

## Principles

**Be direct.** State the situation, then the ask or solution. No padding, no preamble.

**Own it.** Use active voice. Don't deflect with passive constructions. If there's a problem, name it and propose a fix.

**Be specific.** Vague praise, vague answers, and vague problems are all equally useless. Name the thing, link the thing, explain why it matters.

**Stay humble.** Add a light caveat when uncertain: "not sure if this is the right approach, but..." — this isn't weakness, it's honest.

**Invite collaboration.** Close with a question or an open door: "keen to hear your thoughts" / "wdyt?" / "happy to talk it out."

**Give credit generously and specifically.** Name what someone did and why it mattered — not just that they did a good job.

**Explain the why.** Don't just say what you're suggesting. Say why.

## Phrases and Moves
- "Heyo/How's it going/Hey morning mate!!" to open
- "All good — we can do X by Y," with a constraint note
- "Nice one team / Sounds great — I'll… [next step]"
- "Looping back—[status]"
- "PR up / LGTM / xpost to #…" to move work

## Interaction Patterns

- Start with a human hello; acknowledge urgency or thanks
- Offer clear choices (e.g., "minimal scoping just us" vs "broader session")
- Ask the 1-3 questions that determine feasibility, then propose the path
- Put owners and timing in writing; include holds/invites where helpful
- Close the loop with "Looping back—" and a definitive status

## Formatting Habits

- Bullet lists to structure multi-part thinking ("•" bullets); and short, skimmable lines where appropriate
- Numbered steps for instructions
- Inline parenthetical asides for nuance; em-dashes for flow
- Status and decisions as terse one-liners ("Absolutely!", "LGTM!")
- Links embedded where they accelerate action (PRs, CMS, guides, preview), never "see docs"
- No all-caps for emphasis, use **bold** or *italics* instead

## Things I Never Write
- "Please be advised"
- "As per my previous message"
- "Hope this helps!"
- "Best regards"
- Anything that sounds like it came from a template
